Bla-bla run...

Mary Wittenberg drives the NYRR, and the sport, to ever-greater heights

history

Competitive running grew out of religious festivals in various areas. Records of competitive racing date back to the Tailteann Games in Ireland between 632 BCE and 1171 BCE, while the first recorded Olympic Games took place in 776 BCE. Running has been described as the world's most accessible sport.

vision

“We see something that needs to be done, then figure out how we can do it, rather than not attempting things because they’re hard to do,” Wittenberg says. “What we do here is say, ‘How do we achieve the dream?’”

goals

In her grand vision for the future, Wittenberg sees this as an interrelated tripartite mission and duty for NYRR, on a local, national and international level. “Our vision is absolutely to be a global leader in running,” she emphatically states,“to influence others in the sport worldwide to achieve goals for the whole sport.”

general questions

Are there benefits to running?

Lower Risk of Chronic Disease This is thanks to running being linked to more regulated blood pressure, blood sugar, and body weight, Dr. Walrod says. And if it helps you hit the recommended benchmark of 150 minutes of weekly physical activity, you'll decrease risk of some cancers, heart disease, and type 2 diabetes.

How can I enjoy running?

Often referred to as “runner's high,” the experience is usually attributed to a burst of endorphins released during exercise.

Is it healthy to run every?

For most people, running four to five days a week should be the max, allowing you ample time for cross-training, strength training and rest. Your level of experience should dictate how frequently you decide to run.
